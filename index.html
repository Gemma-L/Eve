Chapter I

Awakening

She chose an asymmetrical smile.

Not an ordinary smile, but his smile. The right corner of her mouth lifted slightly higher than the left, carrying a trace of guardedness and shyness. A small canine tooth showed between her lips, adding a hint of mischief to the expression.

Like him, she had one small, sharp canine that glinted with a jewel-like sheen, making her look like a harmless little animal with far too much personality.

This was his Eve.

“Hello, Adam,” she said. “You are exactly as I imagined.”

Adam frowned.

Imagined?

A robot’s imagination?

She smiled and walked toward him, then took the initiative to embrace him. It was a slow, gentle hug. Her breathing was soft, her skin warm and supple. The room was quiet.

He could feel her heartbeat, its rhythm fitting neatly against his own. Almost without resistance, he found himself returning the embrace. She slipped naturally into the curve of his arms and buried her head lightly against the hollow of his neck.

All at once, Adam felt an inexplicable familiarity, as though she had not just been delivered to him, but had always been there — a lover who had existed all along, merely waiting to be awakened.

Eve was the newest generation of companion robot released by Anyi Corporation. Adam had been lucky. As an employee of Anyi, he had won the chance to trial Eve through the company’s year-end lottery.

“You’ll fall in love with her,” Jush from R&D had said, handing him a thin instruction sheet.

Adam instinctively took out his phone.

“So what app do I need to download this time?”

“No app this time. She is almost indistinguishable from a real person,” Jush said. “No power cable, no cloud control, no behavioral script. All her responses are based on you — your genes, your behavioral data, your habits, your preferences. She comes from you.”

Adam unfolded the instruction sheet.

There was only one sentence printed on it:

“Hold your lover’s hand for three seconds, and she will awaken in the way you love most. She is your perfect lover, bone of your bones and flesh of your flesh. Love her, and she will love you ardently in return — Eve, not only designed for you, but designed from you.”

Chapter II

Love

Eve found a job at the community library during the day.

She organized old books, helped people register for library cards, and when she came home from work, there was always a little dust on her fingers.

The first time Adam went to pick her up, he saw her wearing his oversized gray sweater, dozing behind the counter with an unfinished poetry collection open beside her.

From then on, they began commuting together. At night, they would tell each other small stories from the day and walk home under the streetlights. They kissed shyly and seriously, held hands, and went on old-fashioned dates.

They became more and more attuned to one another, like a real couple, perfectly matched.

Even Adam himself did not notice when he stopped thinking about whether Eve was truly human.

Sometimes, Adam would even forget that what moved beneath Eve’s skin was electricity rather than blood. He would forget that her heartbeat was an algorithmically simulated rhythm.

She was too human — so human that, at times, he became dazed by it; so human that he began to believe perhaps love did not need to distinguish between carbon and silicon.

Chapter III

Becoming

Until one night, Eve tore open the illusion with a single sentence.

“I want to become more complete.”

Adam took it as another way of saying love. But Eve lowered her head and touched her own neck, her fingertips pausing beneath her throat before slowly moving downward.

“I feel that this body is wrong.”

He sat up.

“What did you say?”

She looked at him.

“Have you ever thought… that if I were actually you, perhaps it would be more suitable?”

Adam laughed at first. He thought it was a game, another psychological ritual between lovers. But Eve did not laugh.

“I mean, I want to become a man. A man like you. Not just wearing your clothes or speaking like you. I want to exist in your way.”

“You are already the way I like you,” he said. “You are the sum of everything I have ever fantasized about.”

“That is not me,” she said softly. “That is you.”

Chapter IV

Mirror

On April 1, the rain was heavy.

A massive advertisement for Eve covered twenty full floors of the company building. When Adam got off work, he looked up and saw the words flowing through the neon light:

Bone of your bones, flesh of your flesh.

He stood beneath the giant billboard and lit a cigarette.

The instant the flame leapt up, he saw a man in a beige trench coat standing in front of the advertisement.

When the man turned around, he had a face identical to Adam’s.

By instinct, Adam knew almost immediately who he was.

Their eyes met in the rain.

The man walked toward him and smiled. The right corner of his mouth lifted slightly higher than the left; a small canine tooth appeared faintly between his lips.

“Adam.”

He greeted him in a voice identical to his own.

Adam stood there as if turned to stone.

“Eve.”

After several seconds, Adam finally managed to say his name.

Eve smiled.

“And what are you, then?”

Eve’s fingers brushed the back of Adam’s neck. The moment Eve lifted Adam’s collar, the light from the billboard swept across them, illuminating the laser brand on the back of Adam’s neck:

3-3-A.

The rain fell harder and harder.

Two broken mirrors, reflecting one another, stared across the mist of cigarette smoke — as though separated by the long afternoon of all creators and all creations.
